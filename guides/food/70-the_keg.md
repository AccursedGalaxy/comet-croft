# Brewing

Every good homestead ends up with a cellar, and every good cellar ends up with a keg.

Grow **hops**, brew ales, cider, stouts and stranger things — each glass carries its own small comfort.

@recipe alcocraftplus:spruce_keg + alcocraftplus:spruce_mug
Any wood's slabs and planks will do, but the keg wants a **hop** at its heart — grow those first. Mugs come four at a time; you'll want the spares.

@recipe alcocraftplus:hop_seeds
A hop crafts straight into seeds, so one good harvest keeps the hop garden going on its own.

@smelting alcocraftplus:dry_seeds
Wheat seeds, toasted in the furnace. Most brews ask for these alongside hops, so keep a jar of them by the keg.

# What's on tap

Each brew is a keg recipe of its own — hops, dry seeds, and something that gives it character. And remember the hops in your garden answer to the year like any other crop; the **Seasons** chapter picks up that thread.
