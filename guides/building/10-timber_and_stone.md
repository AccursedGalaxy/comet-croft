# Felling trees

Break one log and the whole tree comes down, leaves and all. No floating trunks, no ladder of dirt.

When you want just the one log — a treehouse, a trim, a beam — sneak while you chop and the tree stands.

@recipe minecraft:iron_axe
Timber work goes best with a proper axe in hand. Iron is cheap once you've a vein or two banked, so keep a spare by the door.

# Mining veins

Spot an ore, hold **middle mouse** while you break it, and the entire vein follows it out of the wall.

Two small rules: you need the right tool for the ore, and there's a short breather between veins. Everything still costs its honest durability — bring a spare pickaxe for the big coal seams.

@recipe minecraft:stonecutter
Feed the stonecutter your mined stone and pick the exact cut — stairs, slabs, bricks — one block in, one block out, nothing wasted. When the chests are heavy with logs and cut stone, read on: *A Prettier Croft* shows you what to make of them.
