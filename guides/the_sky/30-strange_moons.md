# Strange moons

Most nights the moon is just the moon. Perhaps one night in twenty it rises changed: blood-red, pale white, swollen huge, or dark as an eclipse.

Each one bends the night in its own way — some make the dark more dangerous, some more generous. If the sky looks wrong at sundown, step out carefully, or watch from a window with something warm.

None of them will keep you from your bed. If you'd rather not meet one, sleep it away — every strange moon lets you.
