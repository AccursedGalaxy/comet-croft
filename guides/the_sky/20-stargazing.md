# The night sky

On a clear night, look up through a spyglass. The stars out here are real: constellations wheel overhead through the year, and the brighter wanderers among them are planets.

Best of all, you can trace new constellations yourself and give them names. The sky remembers them.

Amethyst grows inside hollow geodes underground. Watch cave walls for patches of smooth basalt and calcite — crack through and the purple is inside. The clusters regrow, so one geode is plenty.

# Drawing your own

Zoom in with the spyglass, then press your pick-block key (middle click) to switch it between modes. In constellation mode, hold left click and drag lines from star to star. In star mode you can select a single star or planet instead.

Once something is selected, type **/sga:name** followed by a name to christen it, or **/sga:info** to learn a little about it.

# A shared sky

Everyone on the server sees the same stars, but the constellations you draw start out yours alone. Select one and type **/sga:share** to get a code a friend can use to add it to their own sky.

When you've had your fill of the stars, keep an eye on the moon. Some nights it rises changed — the next page, **Strange Moons**, covers that.
